BOSH Guidelines for Engineers: Safety MEXEasures in the Midst of CrICEsis

SPEAKER

Mr. Fortunato D. Barroga Jr.

Safety Officer at Al Ahlia

Insights...

The seminar focuses on imparting essential knowledge and guidelines related to occupational safety and health (OSH) for engineers. BOSH, or Basic Occupational Safety and Health, provides a comprehensive framework for ensuring the well-being of individuals in the workplace. In the context of this seminar, the emphasis is placed on the unique challenges engineers face during crises, referred to as "CrICEsis," and the implementation of MEXEasures (Measures for Excellence in Engineering Safety). Participants will gain insights into practical safety measures, crisis management strategies, and the integration of safety protocols into engineering practices, thereby fostering a culture of safety and resilience in the face of challenges. This seminar aims to equip engineers with the necessary tools to navigate safety concerns effectively and contribute to a secure and thriving work environment.

The seminar offers Mechatronics Engineering students valuable insights into implementing safety measures in their field. By understanding BOSH guidelines and crisis management strategies, students can enhance workplace safety, mitigate risks, and contribute to creating a secure environment for the integration of mechatronic systems. This knowledge ensures a foundation for responsible engineering practices, aligning with the principles of occupational safety and health, and ultimately, promoting the well-being of both individuals and mechatronic systems in various professional settings.
